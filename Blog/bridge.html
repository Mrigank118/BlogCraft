Best SpeedBridging techniques in Minecraft

Hooola! Wanna learn something about Speed Bridging, Right choice?


First off! What is Speed Bridging ?

Speedbridging in Minecraft is a way to cross large gaps between islands or players by moving backwards at a constant speed while repeatedly pressing and releasing the shift key, also known as the sneak key.

Am I not Speed Bridging? how should I?

Well, Yes, Idk. Speed Bridging is not your normal continuous shift key press while placing blocks. Some players even remain under misconception of being able to speed bridge.
Dont try using those professional bridging techniques all at once, try the most common ones.

Here's an order of bridges from Novice to Pro!


A-S-D Sneaking

This might not be regarded as a Speed brige but it is faster than the normal straight sneaking by milliseconds. All you need to do is, instead of using only your 'S' key to move backwards you press both 'A' and 'S' or 'D' and 'S' while maintaing a rough angle of 45 degree with the block. It is found to be faster by 0.5 blocks per second.



Ninja Bridging

Fast Speed Bridge and also the most important to master. Without Ninja Bridge it is almost impossible to move ahead on this list. It requires the most basic technique required for every speed bridge.

1) To Ninja Bridge just align yourself in straight line with the blocks.
2) Now, press shift and place a block.
3) But while you placed a block, you need to unshift at the start of the block and shift at the end of it.
4) Next, Slowly repeat. It would be very hard in the beginning but you'll get used to the rythem.



Diagonal Speed bridge

Another useful rather more practical bridge because not ever time your destination will be in a straight path but diagonal. It is just a zig-zag.
To do it put your crosshair a little to the right side of the edge of a block, then double click, unshift, walk to the edge, shift again, and keep going through the same process.

Its main utility is bedwars where you need to go to a base present at the diagonal.

Onestack

It's one of the most useful bridge for Minecraft Bedwars and Skywars. Basically instead of a straight path a staircase is be made. It is better than just bridging horizontally then vertically because the sum of 2 sides of a triangle is always greater than the hypotenuse , MATHS :)

The speed is not as fast as bridging straight, While it speed appears to be slow, it takes you to height from which you can jump the gap. Saving Time! it is pretty usefull and a must learn.



Breezily Bridging

Ok brezzily is quite hard and also different from the rest of the bridging techniques. In Breezily you dont need to Sneak and Unsneak evertime but instead the player instead of going straight backwards moves right-D and left-A on the block

The left and right movement is done to reduce the speed more likely distance travelled. While moving with Unshift is too fast, Shift too slow, breezily has the perfect speed.



Telly Bridging

Quite Hard what you need to do is to make a jump after placing a block, look sideways(away from block end) and then look back at the block to place the next block.



~ All the speeds mentioned are the near max and it depends on players. The speed how fast a player is able to place a block and not directly how many blocks he travels in a second.
